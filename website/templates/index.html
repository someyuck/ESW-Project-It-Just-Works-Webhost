About Our Robot

We all know robots are becoming increasingly important in our lives,doing tasks from delivering packages to exploring unknown places.But there’s a big challenge they face: how to move around without bumping into things. Our project, “Obstacle avoidance for ground robots”,is all about solving this problem. We want to make sure that robots can move around safely and efficiently. We have used a simple algorithm for avoiding obstacles while navigating it's path. This method offers a versatile way to plan paths while avoiding collisions

Some other Key Points:
Safety enhancement: Ensuring that robots can navigate around obstacles in real-time,without collisions,is essential to prevent accidents and damage.

Autonomous Exploration: Autonomous obstacle avoidance is vital in enabling these robots to navigate challenging and unknown terrains effectively for crucial applications such as search and rescue, and delivering items to their desired location without damaging the item itself.

Timeline

This is the basic timeline of how we went about doing this project over the span of 10 weeks.

Week 1-2

Hardware testing and Interfacing

  • We tested sensors (ultrasonic sensors) and actuators for any errors present in their reading and accordingly calibrating our readings in code, ensuring their reliability for obstacle avoidance. We established communication between sensors and the central processing unit.

Week 3-4

Assembly of robot & algorithm implementation

  • We assembled the robot - placing sensors in correct orientation and assembling wheels. The algorithm implementation involved creating a seamless connection between sensor inputs and decision-making processes (we decided to keep out algorithm fairly simple and straight forward to decrease the processing time), optimizing the performance of our obstacle avoiding bot.

Week 5-6

Testing Robot for sensor Input & motion control

  • Our testing focused on validating sensor inputs for precise obstacle detection, while motion control was rigorously assessed to ensure seamless navigation. This meticulous process ensures the reliability and effectiveness of our robot's sensor-based obstacle avoidance and motion control mechanisms.

Week 7-8

Improving on Software & testing

  • We first just used 3 ultrasonic sensors but we were not getting enough information about the surroundings for efficient navigation so we further integrated two more ultrasonic sensors and updated the code accordingly - finally testing the final model.

Week 8-10

Final testing for edge cases

  • In the final testing phase, our focus extended to handling edge cases, rigorously evaluating the robot's response to diverse and challenging scenarios. This comprehensive assessment ensures the robustness of our system, guaranteeing reliable performance even in unconventional situations for the successful deployment of our autonomous obstacle avoidance car project.
